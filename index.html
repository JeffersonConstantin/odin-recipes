Recipes For Fullstack Developers

Some full stack web developers will proudly orchestrate microservices, tame Kubernetes clusters, and refactor a monolith before lunch—but ask them to cook actual spaghetti and suddenly they’re debugging the “pasta pipeline.” They approach a kitchen like it’s a production outage: aggressively Googling “how to boil water,” optimizing the heat settings for “performance,” and insisting the smoke alarm is just sending “real-time notifications.” If a recipe says “season to taste,” they open 47 tabs, compare Stack Overflow answers, and still deploy a grilled cheese that’s somehow both burnt and undercooked—proof that even in full stack mode, the kitchen is still in beta.

But worry not me hearties,I am here to save the day (or night)!!!



Here are three recipes for my culinarily handicapped fellow-programmers to dish out (pun intended) on the extremely rare chance that we have some guests coming over and we are in charge of their nourishment! (God Forbid!!).

Without furter ado, Ladies and Gents, I present to you:
